Experiment 1: Acid-Base Titration

Objective: To determine the concentration of an acid using a base of known concentration.

Materials: Burette, pipette, conical flask, phenolphthalein indicator, acid, base.

Procedure:

  1. Fill the burette with the base solution.
  2. Pipette 10 mL of the acid into a conical flask.
  3. Add 2-3 drops of phenolphthalein indicator to the acid.
  4. Titrate the acid with the base until the solution turns pink.
  5. Record the volume of base used.

IoT Integration:

pH: 8.5 (Alkaline)
Temperature: 25¬∞C
Gas Safety: Safe

Experiment 4: Rusting of Iron

Objective: To investigate the conditions required for rusting of iron.

Materials: Iron nails, test tubes, water, oil, calcium chloride (drying agent).

Procedure:

  1. Place iron nails in three test tubes:
    • Tube 1: Nail exposed to air and water.
    • Tube 2: Nail exposed to air only (add calcium chloride to absorb moisture).
    • Tube 3: Nail submerged in boiled water covered with oil to exclude air.
  2. Observe the nails over several days for rust formation.
